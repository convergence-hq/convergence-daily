Signals

Commercial pilot deployment in quarterly IR disclosures: Tesla, Figure/BMW, Apptronik/Mercedes-Benz, Symbotic customers, and Serve Robotics are the leading public indicators of embodied robotics moving from demos toward revenue inflection. Pilots now appear in quarterly earnings calls and formal commercial agreements — a transition from press-release theater to falsifiable data.

Figure AI financing or IPO as public comparable reset: Figure AI's large private funding round, OpenAI collaboration, BMW commercial agreement, and Figure 02 launch make Figure financing or IPO timing the central catalyst for repricing public humanoid-robotics comparables including TSLA (Optimus lens) and automation incumbents. Private-to-public catalyst clock is running; timing is the unknown.

Harmonic-drive and rare-earth magnet supply as cost-curve governor: Precision actuators, strain-wave gearing, and neodymium magnet availability are practical bottlenecks for humanoid unit cost and production volume. Every major developer publicly cites component cost as a scaling constraint. Normalization or acceleration of the actuator supply curve is the primary unknown in cost-per-unit projections and the variable the options market is not pricing.

Options market at MEDIUM/growing — not pricing catalyst sequencing or supply constraint: TSLA convergence score 0.50 (MEDIUM/growing), ISRG at 0.40. Scanner records 13 contracts across the full robotics-humanoid theme versus 35 in AI Infrastructure and 28 in GLP-1. Last scanned TSLA structure: 455 strike call, 20.3% OTM, 44.4% IV, asymmetry score 6.13 — reflecting baseline TSLA volatility, not a humanoid-specific catalyst premium. Scanner data from 2026-04-28; specific contract expired May 29.

What would falsify it

Figure AI postpones an IPO or primary financing catalyst past Q2 2026 while BMW deployment remains limited to non-revenue pilots — removes the private-to-public catalyst clock. Tesla shelves Optimus production beyond internal-use scope through 2027 or removes Optimus production milestones from quarterly disclosure — removes the highest-visibility public data point. Harmonic-drive, actuator, and rare-earth magnet suppliers disclose broad lead-time normalization while humanoid developers stop citing component cost or supply as a scaling constraint — actuator bottleneck thesis is wrong. BMW, Mercedes-Benz, or another top-ten auto OEM cancels a humanoid pilot citing unit economics, safety acceptance, or reliability as the primary reason — demand-side invalidation.
